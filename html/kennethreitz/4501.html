[[Category:Puritan_Great_Migration]] {{Puritan Great Migration}} [[Category:Braintree%2C_Massachusetts]] == Biography == William Allis was born 1613 probably in Dagenham, Essex, England, butpossibly London. He emmigrated to Mass. about 1635. Some sources say he was with Winthrop's fleet in 1630 on the Mayflower in its third voyage. He took the Freeman's Oath at Braitree, Mass. in 1640. His first wife was Mary, born 1614 in Braintree, Essex, England, whom he marriedabout 1641 in Braintree, Norfolk, Mass., and they had eight children.His second wife was Mary Brownson, whom he married June 25, 1678 in Hatfield, Hampshire. Mass. He died on 06 September 1678, and his will was probated on 24 September 1678. === Marriage === : Husband: [[Allis-37|Lt. [[Category:Puritan_Great_Migration]] {{Puritan Great Migration|needs=Research|needs1=Biography}} == Biography == :Mary Brownson was baptized December 12, 1627 at Earls Colne, Essex, England.<ref> Baptism register 1620 to 1639 (D/P209/1/2) 12.10.1627 (Friday 12 October 1627) document 6702624 "Mary daughter of Jn Brownson and Fran Brownson christened 12.10.1627" </ref>the daughter of John Bronson<ref name=Sibley>[https://archive.org/stream/bronsonlineage1600sibl#page/11/mode/1up| Sibley, Harriet Bronson. ''"Bronson Lineage"'' 1636-1917] Dallas, Oregon. 1917, p. 11</ref> In 1635 she accompanied her parents to Massachussets, probablyon the "Defense" with other people from Earls Colne. She grew up in Hartford and married John Wyatt by 1647, John Graves perhaps in 1671, William Allis in 1679 and Samuel Gaylord in 1681. She died after 1694. Death:: She was living in 1694.<ref> Jacobus, Donald Lines . The ancestry of Lorenzo Ackley & his wife Emma Arabella Bosworth / compiled and published by N. Grier Parke, II; (Woodstock, Vt. : Elm Tree Press, 1960.)</ref> Jacobus does not state why he knows this, but somewhere there isa document signed or acknowledged by her in 1694. : After 22 SEP 1689 (the date of husband Samuel's will) :Find a grave says she died 1688, but she was alive still then.:Apparent incorrect report of being killed by Indians in the HatfieldMassacre on September 19, 1677. Her husband John Graves was killed in the massacre.
