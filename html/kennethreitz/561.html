'''Last Will and Testament'''<br/>The Last Will & Testamt of Elizabeth Moricke I Elizabeth moricke widdow of Jno morricke deseased at Hingham, But now Dwellinge in Roxbury beinge in food & perfect memory & through Gods mercy Not deprived of mywill & sesces, Doe in this my Last Will & testamt take Robert Hull ofBoston to be my executor and Leonard fellowes of Great Bowd in in oldengland with ____ of my goods & chattlesw: & allso that I Doe give unto William ffellowes of Ipswidge ten pounds: & to Richard fellowes of Connecticut tenn pounds: & allso to Samuell ffellowes of Salisbury tenn pounds [9th line]& to William healy of Roxberry I give eight pounds : & to Mr Eliot of Roxbury twenty Shill<sup>s</sup>, & to Mr Danforth of Roxbery tenn shillings : & to Mr. Hubbert the minister of Hingham tenn shillings : & to Ann Hillard of Hingham: on shillinge & to John Scath on shilling & to Ann Scath on shillinge : & allso I desire my Loving freinds James Johnson & Jn<sup>o</sup> Hull of Boston to oversee this my Last will, & I give unto each one of them five shilings & to this I set my Hand this 14th of march 1649: that eight pound ____ ___ inthe 9th Line is foure pound to himselfe & the other foure between thetwo eldest children of the sd William Healy : Item I give to my sister grace Allan in Lincktonshire five pound:" Signed by mark.
